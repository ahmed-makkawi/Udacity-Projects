Introduction¶

On daily basis, people tend to make reservations for doctors appointments before going to avoid waiting a long time. Some people tend to reserve an appointment and never show up, which affects doctors utilization with patients.

In this project, a dataset of 100K patients were collected in Brazil in 2016. We will try to understand the charactraristics of no show patients by investigating the dataset and try to predict if a patient will show or not show on his/her reserved appointment.

Questions¶

1. is there a relation between neighbourhood and no show ?

2. What are the charactaristics of no show hipertension patients ?

3. Which age category has the highest no show proportion ?

Conclusions¶

Results¶

1. From our analysis above, we can conclude that some neighbourhoods tend to not show to appointments higher than other neighbourhoods. So for example if the patient is living in SANTOS DUMONT, there is a 40.6% chance they will not show up in their next appointment. The detailed percentages are available in the sortedNeighbourList list.

2. Next analyzed the hipertension patients from several prespectives and found that 45% of them do not show to appointments. The previous analysis led us to dig deeper in these type of patients charactarstics, which led to that the average age of hipertension patients who do not show is 60 years old.

3. Next, we studied the no show only dataset for hipertension patients regarding their diabetic condition. It was found that 30% of hipertension patients have diabetes as well, while only 1% of no hipertension patients have diabetes.

4. The highest proportion of no show for age category was for teenagers and 20s with 33% of no show.

Limitations¶

1. Most of our data is categorical which is limiting our search results.

2. The appointment day value is recorded as same hour and min and second for all patients, which might lead to incorrect reservations made but were not visible.

3. sms received field is not clear, when the sms was received for example or was it a reminder on the same reservation day, the graphs are showing that the percentage of no show patients actually received an sms not the opposite.
